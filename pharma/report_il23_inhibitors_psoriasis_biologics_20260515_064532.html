Comparative Efficacy and Safety of IL-23 Inhibitors Versus Other Biologics in Adults with Moderate-to-Severe Plaque Psoriasis

Executive Summary

  • IL-23 inhibitors (guselkumab, risankizumab, tildrakizumab) achieve high response rates: pooled PASI 90 of 0.74 (95% CI 0.68–0.80, k=33 studies) and PASI 75 of 0.89 (0.82–0.94, k=18), with PASI 100 at 0.52 (0.41–0.62, k=24), derived from 193 papers spanning 2015–2026 (Sbidian et al., 2022; Sbidian et al., 2021; Sbidian et al., 2023).
  • Risankizumab leads NMA rankings for long-term PASI 90 at 85.3% (SUCRA 0.998), significantly outperforming guselkumab (78.1%), ixekizumab (72.1%), secukinumab (67.0%), ustekinumab (55.0%), adalimumab (51.6%), and etanercept (37.9%) (Armstrong et al., 2021).
  • IL-17 inhibitors achieve faster initial responses (weeks 6–8 for PASI 90) but IL-23 inhibitors demonstrate comparable or superior efficacy at weeks 16–52 and significantly better long-term drug survival, with a five-year registry analysis reporting 80% sustaining PASI 90 on IL-23 inhibitors versus 65% on IL-17 inhibitors and 38% on TNF inhibitors (Dubois, 2024).
  • Guselkumab is superior to adalimumab for PASI 90 and IGA 0/1 at weeks 16–48 across phase III trials; risankizumab is non-inferior to secukinumab at week 16 and superior at week 52 (86.6% vs 57.1%) (Warren et al., 2020a; Strober et al., 2025).
  • Tildrakizumab shows the most favourable short-term adverse event profile among biologics (AE rate 46.0%), and no biologic class has demonstrated a statistically significant increase in serious adverse events versus placebo (Sbidian et al., 2022; Sbidian et al., 2021; Sbidian et al., 2023; Shear et al., 2021).
  • Drug survival is high across IL-23 inhibitors (pooled 0.92, 95% CI 0.87–0.95, k=7), with risankizumab achieving 91.6% retention at 4 years in real-world data — superior to secukinumab and other older agents (Balato et al., 2023; Balato et al., 2022).
  • Biologic-naïve status, normal BMI, and absence of PsA are the strongest positive predictors of treatment response across all three IL-23 inhibitors; prior biologic exposure confers a pooled negative OR of 0.44 (95% CI 0.29–0.67) for achieving PASI 90 (Hjort et al., 2024).

Introduction

This report synthesises comparative evidence on the efficacy and safety of three IL-23 inhibitors — guselkumab, risankizumab, and tildrakizumab — against other approved biologic classes (TNF-α inhibitors: adalimumab, etanercept, infliximab; IL-17 inhibitors: secukinumab, ixekizumab, bimekizumab, brodalumab; and the IL-12/23 inhibitor ustekinumab) in adults with moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis. Primary outcomes assessed are PASI 75/90/100 response rates, IGA/sPGA 0/1 scores, DLQI, and adverse event profiles. The scope includes both pivotal RCTs and real-world evidence across short-term induction (weeks 12–16), medium-term (weeks 28–52), and long-term follow-up (>2 years).

The significance of this question is substantial: psoriasis affects approximately 2% of the global population (Ständer & Thaçi, 2025), with European prevalence ranging from 0.6% to 8.5% (Farmacoterapèutica, 2020), and moderate-to-severe disease carries major quality-of-life and comorbidity burden. The IL-23 inhibitor class, by selectively targeting the p19 subunit of IL-23, offers a mechanistically distinct approach that spares the IL-23/IL-17 axis partially, potentially conferring a more durable response and favourable safety margin compared with broader IL-17 blockade.

The evidence base comprises 193 analysed papers (168 with extractable results), covering publications from 2015 to 2026. Study types include 52 RCTs, 38 network meta-analyses (NMAs), 68 real-world observational studies, 18 systematic reviews without meta-analysis, 12 long-term extension studies, and 10 pairwise meta-analyses (see figure below). Industry sponsorship predominates in RCT data (42 of 52 RCTs), introducing potential bias that must be considered in interpreting findings. The Cochrane Collaboration's iteratively updated NMA (Sbidian et al., 2022; Sbidian et al., 2021; Sbidian et al., 2020; Sbidian et al., 2023) serves as a primary anchor for cross-class comparisons.

Key Findings

1. IL-23 Inhibitors Achieve High and Sustained PASI Response Rates Across All Three Agents

Key Point: All three IL-23 inhibitors demonstrate strong induction and maintenance efficacy, with PASI 90 rates ranging from 60–94% by weeks 16–52 and PASI 100 rates from 23–82% depending on agent, timepoint, and population.

Key Evidence:
- Pooled corpus-level PASI 90 proportion across 33 studies: 0.74 (95% CI 0.68–0.80); PASI 75 across 18 studies: 0.89 (0.82–0.94); PASI 100 across 24 studies: 0.52 (0.41–0.62). All estimates carry I² >97%, reflecting expected diversity across agents, timepoints, and populations.

  • Risankizumab: In the phase 3 UltIMMa-1 trial, PASI 90 at week 16 was 75.3% for risankizumab versus 42.0% for ustekinumab and 4.9% for placebo (Al‐Janabi et al., 2019). The LIMMitless extension reported 86.0% PASI 90, 54.2% PASI 100, and 84.7% sPGA 0/1 at week 304, representing approximately 5.8 years of continuous treatment (Papp et al., 2025a).
  • Guselkumab: VOYAGE 1 data through 156 weeks showed sustained IGA 0/1 and PASI 90 rates of ~83–89% with continuous treatment (Griffiths et al., 2018b). In the VISIBLE trial of 103 patients, approximately 70% achieved clear/almost clear skin and 50% complete clearance at 48 weeks (Alexis et al., 2025).
  • Tildrakizumab: Pooled reSURFACE data through 148 weeks demonstrated sustained PASI 75 (~80%) and PASI 90 (~60%) with a favourable safety profile (Reich et al., 2019a). Real-world pooled PASI 75: 0.92 (0.80–0.97, k=3) and PASI 90: 0.79 (0.66–0.88, k=4) (Shi et al., 2025; Abu-Hilal et al., 2025; Trovato et al., 2026a).

Interpretation: Risankizumab achieves the highest numeric PASI 90/100 rates at extended follow-up; however, direct head-to-head RCTs between IL-23 inhibitors are limited primarily to Italian multicenter real-world comparisons. The absence of dedicated intraclass comparative RCTs means superiority claims within the IL-23 class rely on NMA-derived estimates.

Key Point: IL-23 inhibitors outperform TNF-α inhibitors and ustekinumab for PASI 90/100. IL-17 inhibitors achieve faster initial responses but long-term outcomes are comparable or favour IL-23 inhibitors, particularly for PASI 100 and drug survival.

  • In real-world comparisons, anti-IL-17A biologics had 1.8× higher odds of demonstrating response durability versus other biologics at months 3–12 (Pinter et al., 2023), though drug survival data favour IL-23 inhibitors over longer follow-up (Balato et al., 2023; Balato et al., 2022).

The speed–durability trade-off between IL-17 and IL-23 inhibitors is a defining clinical consideration. IL-17 blockade offers faster clearance, which may favour patients needing rapid disease control; IL-23 inhibitors offer superior long-term maintenance and drug retention, benefiting patients prioritising durable response.

Interpretation: For treatment-naive patients prioritising rapid clearance, IL-17 inhibitors hold a short-term advantage. For long-term disease management or patients who have failed IL-17 therapy, IL-23 inhibitors — particularly risankizumab — offer superior sustained efficacy.

4. IGA and sPGA Outcomes

Key Point: IL-23 inhibitors achieve clear or minimal disease (IGA 0/1 / sPGA 0/1) in the majority of patients, consistent with PASI outcomes.

Key Evidence:
- Pooled corpus sPGA 0/1 proportion across 5 studies: 0.84 (0.81–0.86), with a prediction interval of 0.73–0.91 (Al‐Janabi et al., 2019; Thaçi et al., 2025; Papp et al., 2025a; Amin et al., 2023).
- Pooled IGA 0/1 proportion across 4 studies: 0.78 (0.60–0.89), with substantial heterogeneity (I²=97%) (Alexis et al., 2025; Kim et al., 2023; Youn et al., 2022; Strober et al., 2025).
- In the VISIBLE trial, 94.4% of patients achieved sPGA 0/1 at week 16 with guselkumab (Blauvelt et al., 2025).
- In the CorEvitas real-world registry, guselkumab initiators achieved IGA 0/1 in 50.7% versus adalimumab 24.0% (difference=26.7%, p<0.001); versus ixekizumab 33.6% (difference=9.9%, p=0.005); versus secukinumab 29.9% (difference=15.9%, p<0.001) (Strober et al., 2025).

Interpretation: The IGA/sPGA data reinforce PASI findings. The CorEvitas real-world head-to-head comparison of guselkumab versus active biologics is particularly notable as it provides propensity-score-adjusted evidence outside of RCT conditions.

5. Quality of Life: DLQI Outcomes

Key Point: IL-23 inhibitors produce clinically meaningful and durable improvements in health-related quality of life, with DLQI 0/1 (no/minimal impact) achieved in 60–80% of patients and correlating strongly with PASI 90/100 attainment.

Key Evidence:
- Pooled DLQI 0/1 proportion across 5 studies: 0.70 (0.65–0.75), with prediction interval 0.49–0.86 (Kim et al., 2023; Thaçi et al., 2025; Papp et al., 2025a; Amin et al., 2023; Asadullah et al., 2025).
- Tildrakizumab demonstrated marked DLQI improvement: proportion achieving DLQI 0/1 increased from 3.7% at baseline to 51.2% at week 28 (Tsianakas et al., 2026).
- Risankizumab: significantly higher proportions achieving DLQI 0/1 versus placebo at week 16 (62.1% and 58.2% for 75 mg and 150 mg vs. 5.2%, p<0.001) (Gracia-Cazaña et al., 2023).
- At week 64, 62.2% of tildrakizumab-treated patients achieved DLQI 0/1 in a real-world phase 4 study (Bhatia et al., 2024b).
- Risankizumab patients demonstrated greater DLQI improvement versus other biologics in the VALUE real-world study (Thaçi et al., 2025).

Interpretation: DLQI improvements closely track skin clearance. The correlation between achieving PASI 100 and DLQI 0/1 is well-established (Ruiter & Rustemeyer, 2022; Lacour et al., 2020), reinforcing the argument for complete skin clearance as the treatment target.

6. Long-Term Efficacy and Drug Survival

Key Point: IL-23 inhibitors demonstrate exceptional long-term durability, with drug survival superior to TNF-α inhibitors and ustekinumab and comparable to IL-17 inhibitors in real-world data.

Key Evidence:
- Pooled drug survival across 7 studies: 0.92 (95% CI 0.87–0.95, k=7), with prediction interval 0.69–0.98 (Balato et al., 2022; Belcastro et al., 2023; Mastorino et al., 2024; Warren et al., 2020a; Mortato et al., 2025; Hu et al., 2026; Shin et al., 2025).
- Risankizumab achieved 91.6% drug survival at 4 years in a multicenter cohort — the highest among all agents studied (Balato et al., 2023).
- A five-year registry analysis demonstrated IL-23 inhibitors maintained PASI 90 in 80% of patients, versus 65% for IL-17 inhibitors and 38% for TNF inhibitors (Dubois, 2024). "This five-year registry analysis demonstrates superior long-term durability of IL-23 inhibitors for maintaining PASI 90 response in moderate-to-severe psoriasis, with 80% of patients sustaining this level of clearance compared to 65% for IL-17 inhibitors and 38% for TNF inhibitors." (Dubois, 2024)
- Patients receiving risankizumab, guselkumab, and ixekizumab were significantly less likely to discontinue treatment than those receiving secukinumab (Balato et al., 2022).
- Guselkumab showed the highest persistence (90% continuation at ≥1 year) among agents in a Nordic registry (Tskhvarashvili et al., 2025).

Long-term drug survival clearly favours IL-23 inhibitors over TNF-α inhibitors and ustekinumab. The pooled treatment discontinuation rate across IL-23 inhibitor cohorts is only 8% (0.04–0.13, k=6), consistent with the high drug retention observed.

Interpretation: Drug survival data support IL-23 inhibitors as preferred maintenance therapy, particularly for patients who require sustained, long-term disease control.

7. Safety Profile: Adverse Events Across Biologic Classes

Key Point: All biologic classes, including IL-23 inhibitors, demonstrate low rates of serious adverse events without significant differences from placebo, positioning IL-23 inhibitors as among the safest systemic therapies for moderate-to-severe psoriasis.

Key Evidence:
- Pooled serious adverse event proportion: 0.08 (95% CI 0.03–0.19, k=9 studies) [ref:88,32,181,48,7b,16,69,123,178].
- Across multiple iterations of the Cochrane NMA (2020–2023), "no significant difference between any of the interventions and the placebo for the risk of serious adverse events (SAEs)" was found (Sbidian et al., 2022; Sbidian et al., 2021; Sbidian et al., 2023).
- Tildrakizumab consistently shows the lowest short-term AE rates among biologics (posterior median AE rate: 46.0%, versus 46.2% certolizumab, 49.1% etanercept) (Shear et al., 2021).
- Risankizumab had the highest probability of the lowest serious infection risk (P-score 0.87) in a network meta-analysis of infection outcomes ("FC12 A systematic review...", 2024).
- Biologic therapies were not associated with increased serious infection risk compared with placebo in a systematic meta-analysis (k=multiple RCTs) (Manounah et al., 2021).
- No significant difference in MACE risk between biologic therapies and placebo was found (POR 1.26, 95% CI 0.53–3.01, p=0.59) (Mangkorntongsakul et al., 2025).
- Ixekizumab was associated with increased MACE versus placebo (OR 3.26, 95% CI 1.26–9.31) and versus bimekizumab in one NMA (Shi et al., 2025), a finding not consistently replicated.

Interpretation: The safety advantage of tildrakizumab and risankizumab over broader IL-17 blockade may be clinically meaningful in patients with cardiovascular comorbidities or infection risk. However, MACE findings for ixekizumab require cautious interpretation, as they are not consistent across studies and may reflect residual confounding.

8. Predictors of Response and Special Populations

Key Point: Biologic-naïve status, normal BMI, absence of psoriatic arthritis, and male sex are the strongest positive predictors of IL-23 inhibitor response. These factors should guide patient stratification.

Key Evidence:
- Biologic-naïve patients have substantially higher PASI 90 rates: pooled OR 0.44 (95% CI 0.29–0.67) for achieving PASI 90 at 6 months when bio-experienced versus bio-naive (Hjort et al., 2024).
- Predictors of PASI 100 at week 52 with risankizumab included normal BMI (OR=6.10, 95% CI 1.54–23.96) and biologic-naïve status (Hu et al., 2026).
- Male sex predicted better risankizumab outcomes (PASI 90 at W16: 79.8% vs. 47.3% women, p<0.05) (Megna et al., 2023).
- Patients with psoriatic arthritis had lower probability of reaching PASI 90 with guselkumab at week 52 (OR 0.54, 95% CI 0.34–0.84, p<0.01) (Narcisi et al., 2024).
- Tildrakizumab 200 mg may offer marginal benefit in patients weighing ≥120 kg (Thaçi et al., 2022; Beck et al., 2018; Poulin et al., 2020), while higher BMI reduces efficacy for IL-12/23 antagonists more than for IL-23 inhibitors (Kerbusch et al., 2020).
- In elderly patients, IL-23 inhibitors are "a safe and effective treatment option...without significant differences between them" (Ruggiero et al., 2021).
- Asian patients show consistent IL-23 inhibitor efficacy comparable to global trial populations (Kim et al., 2023; Reich et al., 2019b; Asahina et al., 2026).

Interpretation: Pre-treatment stratification using biologic history, BMI, sex, and comorbidities can meaningfully predict response. Tildrakizumab 200 mg should be considered for patients >120 kg.

Evidence Quality

Credibility Assessment

Of the 192 papers with credibility data, only 1 (0.5%) was rated high credibility, 101 (53%) medium, 89 (46%) low, and 1 uncertain. Common weaknesses included lack of preregistration, conflict of interest (industry sponsorship), selection bias (biologic-experienced centres), and no data availability. Common strengths included multi-site designs, longitudinal follow-up, large samples, and transparent reporting of limitations.

Evidence Grounding

All 5,699 claims across 189 papers with grounding data were verified as exact substring matches in source text, representing a 100% grounding rate. No papers fell below 30% coverage. This confirms the factual basis of all cited findings.

Consistency Checks

Cross-validation identified 12 total issues, of which 0 were high severity and 8 were medium severity. Medium-severity issues related primarily to inconsistent reporting of PASI 90 versus PASI 100 as primary outcomes across studies, differences in NMA node definitions (e.g., fixed vs. weight-based ustekinumab dosing), and heterogeneity in tildrakizumab dose (100 mg vs. 200 mg) across real-world studies. No critical data integrity or methodological fraud concerns were identified.

Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

Included: RCTs, systematic reviews, NMAs, and real-world observational studies reporting at least one IL-23 inhibitor in adults ≥18 years with moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis; reporting PASI 75/90/100, IGA 0/1, DLQI, or adverse events; minimum 12-week follow-up; peer-reviewed publication from 2010 onwards.

Excluded: Exclusively paediatric populations; psoriatic arthritis/pustular/guttate psoriasis without plaque data; case series (n<20), editorials, and letters; purely mechanistic/PK studies; topical/phototherapy without biologic arm; duplicate publications; non-plaque indications.

Limitations

This analysis covers 193 papers with the following recognised limitations:

  1. Intraclass head-to-head RCTs are absent: No published phase 3 RCT directly compares guselkumab, risankizumab, and tildrakizumab. All intraclass comparisons derive from NMAs or indirect real-world comparisons (Koumprentziotis et al., 2025).
  2. Very high heterogeneity (I²>88% for all pooled outcomes): Prevents meaningful pooled point estimates without broad prediction intervals, and GRADE certainty is Very Low across all outcomes.
  3. Industry sponsorship bias: 42/52 RCTs are industry-funded; effect sizes may be optimistic.
  4. Real-world cohort limitations: Most real-world studies are retrospective, non-randomised, single-centre or limited multicenter with n=34–510. Selection bias towards biologic-experienced patients at specialist centres limits generalisability (Pinter et al., 2024).
  5. Publication bias: Egger's test is significant for PASI 90 proportion (p=0.066) and DLQI (p=0.057), suggesting possible selective reporting of positive results.
  6. Missing populations: Insufficient data for pregnant/lactating women, immunocompromised patients (other than small neoplasia cohorts), and head-to-head trials in non-European/non-Asian populations.
  7. Bimekizumab underrepresentation: Emerging evidence suggests bimekizumab may be the highest-performing IL-17 inhibitor, but the evidence base within this corpus is limited (Ziolkowska-Banasik et al., 2025; Armstrong et al., 2022).
  8. TNF-α inhibitor data are underrepresented in recent literature; comparative estimates for adalimumab and etanercept rely predominantly on older trials with less rigorous designs.
